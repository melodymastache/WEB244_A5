Things That Look Like Other Things

A selection of products geared towards those who enjoy things that are not as they seem.
This apple was discovered in the highest mountains of yore by a yodeler named Yanek. Yanek was on a hike with his pet owl, when they stopped for a snack. He used his hunting knife to cut slices from his apple to share his snack with his beloved pet when he noticed an uncanny resemblance between the two. Yanek's asking price for this unique item begins at 70 euros.

An Apple That Looks Like An Owl, €70

This muffin was purposefully baked in likeness of Sir Chow the Third, a Chihuahua from Southern USA with penchant for blueberry muffins. If you are from the south, you may recognize this pupper as the internet sensation that he is. Sir Chow is known for his endearing puppy antics with blueberry muffins. This item's starting price is 50 euros.

A Muffin That Looks Like a Chihuahua, €50

This eggplant was found in the fields of Victoria, BC by a local vegetable farmer who was havesting his fall vegetables. Our seller wishes to remain anonymous, but refers to himself often in the third person by the alias 'Lord Farmer of the West'. He wishes to donate all proceeds of his eggplant emoji sale to Farmers United, a local gathering of farmers who pool together all their money for better farming equipment for those within their coilition in need.

An Eggplant Emoji, €40

This tantalizing twig was found by Wanda Merrison of Oak County in Sheffeild, UK. Wanda was out for a nighttime stroll in a badly lit park when she tripped over a fallen branch. Before she got herself righted, she noticed that the twig closest to her head looked like it was celebrating her fall. Upset at the humor of the twig, Wanda wishes to rid herself of it as soon as possible. Bidding begins as 20 euros, but the seller is willing to negotiate.

A Twig That Is Celebrating, €20

The eggplant sequel to Eggplant Emoji, Eggplant With A Face was found by none other than Lord Farmer of the West himself. He claims to have gone to the local supermarket on his day off and found this eggplant at the bottom of the eggplant bin, thoroughly unnoticed. Lord Farmer wishes to start the bidding at 40 euros for a extremely intriguing source of vitamins.

An Eggplant With A Face, €40

As another contribution to our epic eggplant saga, Squash Swan is from Southern Russia, superfically grown by Dimitri Kalek. To reflect his efforts, this squash is our most expensive one yet, with bidding starting at 60 euros. Kalek hopes to breakeven, as the tools he needed to create this masterpiece were faily pricey.

A Squash Swan, €60

From northern Europe, Ronald the Towel, as he is so aptly named, was found by a jogger in the dumpster near his house. Unfortunately, this jogger states that he cannot care for the poor pup due to living constraints, but wishes for him to find a happy home with someone who will appreciate his towel resemblance. There will be no bidding on this dog, but simply a firmly set price of 3000 euros.

A Dog That Looks Like A Towel, €3000

This fry was found by a casual customer of McDonalds in Korea. We have very little information about our contributor, but they have asked for a set price of 30 euros for their swan fry. A bit steep, but the buyer hopes you will appreciate the keen eye needed to have spotted this extraordinary fry in the first place.

A Fry That Looks Like A Swan, €30

For those rubber duck collectors out there, we have an item of possible interest to you. This tomato rubber duck was grown by Lord Farmer of the West, who is clearly finding an interest in purposeful produce shaping. Lord Farmer is now thinking of focusing solely on this endeavor and is therefore beginning to raise his prices. This rubber duck starts at, accordingly, 50 euros.

A Tomato Rubber Duck, €50

For our last item on the docket, we have the enticing louging legume. This legume is being sold by Lord Farmer as well for the hefty price of 60 euros. Lord Farmer hopes that his clientele is forgiving as he has been shunned from Farmers United for his attempts to pursue his true dream of shaping produce, and therefore no longer has access to the funds for his budding new career.

A Lounging Legume, €60
